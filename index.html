Automatic Bicycle Shifter

*SJSU ME106: Intro to Mechatronics. Mechatronics, electronic prototyping, solid modeling, scripting (Arduino).

An automated alternative to the traditional bicycle shifting system. Developed from scratch, this design incorporates magnetic sensors and an Arduino microcontroller to listen for changes in the rider's cadence and RPMs. The automatic bicycle shifter uses that information to shift to the optimal gear, performing responsively and robustly even going up steep inclines and at high speeds.

"Segway" Robot Stabilization

*SJSU ME190: Mechatronic Systems Design. State-space modeling, digital controls, MATLAB, Simulink.

Designed and implemented a program that allowed a miniature "Segway" robot to stand up on its own. Wrote scripts in MATLAB and designed a controller in Simulink, both of which worked together to utilize on-board sensors to listen for and respond to destabilizing forces which would otherwise topple the robot.

 Who Am I?

Hi, I'm Reuben. First things first, I earned my B.S. Mechanical Engineering from San Jose State University in May 2017. I've always had a knack for programming, and after years of writing code to spin motors and process data from quadrature encoders, I discovered that I was passionate about it.

While still studying at SJSU, I decided to learn more about programming on my own. I worked my way through YouTube tutorials, 500-page books, auditing Comp-Sci classes, and bugging developer friends for advice to arrive at the skillset I have today. Currently I'm building web applications as a freelance full-stack developer, using HTML, CSS, JavaScript (Vanilla, React, jQuery), Python, and Django. I've got projects in Node, Express, and Mongo in the works. As an engineer, I bring analytical problem-solving and strong teamwork skills to the table.

Outside of being a code monkey, I spend most of my time doing Olympic weightlifting, pretending to be a good cook, and petting my dog/looking at pictures of dogs/pretending I'm a dog to my girlfriend.
